    Background

    In urban spaces, the presence and visibility of minority languages in signage and guidance systems significantly affects the accessibility and inclusiveness of these environments for language minorities .

    In the past, signage systems usually gave priority to mainstream languages, leaving minority languages marginalized or even absent.

    minority language

    There is currently no consensus in the academic community on the definition of "minority language", There are two definitions of minority languages based on two different standards

    According to the demographic definition , a minority language is a language used by a small number of people in a territory . (Owens, 2013)

    According to the definition of ethnic society , minority languages need to meet the following four criteria : "self-classification, common ancestry, unique linguistic, cultural or historical characteristics associated with the language, and the organization of social interaction between language groups." ( Allardt , 1984)

    sign language

    Sign languages (also known as signed languages) are languages that use the visual-manual modality to convey meaning, instead of spoken words.

    Sign languages are full-fledged natural languages with their own grammar and lexicon.

    Wherever communities of people with hearing challenges or people who experience deafness exist, sign languages have developed as useful means of communication and form the core of local deaf cultures.

    signage systems

    As the intersection of culture and society in urban space ( Narindrasani & Fuad, 2020) , signage and guidance systems often play a dual role

    On the one hand, Signage and guidance systems are widely used in public places such as urban streets, shopping malls, hospitals, and stations . They help people locate and identify directions in the environment and reach their destination smoothly.

    On the other hand, Through the selection of language and text, the design of symbols and icons, and the display of history and culture, Signage and guidance systems help people from different backgrounds understand the city’s culture, history, social structure, cultural identity, and values (Ben-Rafael et al., 2006) .

    Functional problem

    The neglect of minority languages hinders their ability to navigate space comfortably and confidently (Landry & Bourhis , 1997) .

    Symbolic problem

    The neglect of minority languages undermines their sense of belonging and identity in public spaces, as well as their sense of being recognized by mainstream society (Dressler, 2015) .

    JSL (Japanese Sign Language) Signage System

    -This study aims to understand how the inclusion of Sign languages in a city's signage affects the wayfinding experience and cultural confidence of language minorities and general public.

    -This study aims to shed light on the role of signage as a tool for social inclusion and help build a more equitable signage system that reflects cultural diversity.
